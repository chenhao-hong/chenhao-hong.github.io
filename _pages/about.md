Hi, I’m **Chenhao Hong**, a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Queen Mary University of London (QMUL), within the School of Engineering and Materials Science (SEMS).

My research lies at the intersection of **soft robotics** and **rehabilitation robotics**, where I develop compliant, intelligent systems that can safely interact with the human body and support healthcare applications.

---

## About Me

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Mechanical Engineering at QMUL. My work focuses on the **design, modeling, and control of soft robotic systems** for rehabilitation and assistive technologies.

My research aims to bridge the gap between **robotics engineering** and **human-centered healthcare**, with the goal of improving patient outcomes through innovative robotic solutions.

My main research interests include:
- Soft actuators and compliant mechanisms  
- Wearable and assistive robotic systems  
- Human-robot interaction  
- Rehabilitation engineering  
- Modeling and control of nonlinear systems  

---

## Research

Soft robots offer unique advantages in safety, adaptability, and compliance, making them ideal for applications involving close interaction with humans.

My current research explores how soft robotic technologies can be applied to **rehabilitation devices**, enabling:
- Safe and adaptive physical assistance  
- Personalized rehabilitation strategies  
- Improved comfort and usability for patients  

This work involves a combination of:
- Mechanical design and prototyping  
- Experimental validation  
- Control system development  
- Interdisciplinary collaboration with biomechanics and healthcare
